World Population Report from 1970 to 2020

Since 1970, with economic development, people's material needs have been well supplied and the population around the world has been increasing.

This World Population Report uses data on the population for each decade from 1970 to 2020 to show visually a map of the world's population distribution, the proportions and changes in the population ratios of different continents from 1970 to 2020, the changes in the population growth rates of the top 8 countries in terms of total population, and the two factors affecting the population compared to the population. This report aims to provide more visual information for those interested in population change.

Distribution

The map mainly shows the number of people per square kilometer in each country in the world. The color value is a channel, the darker the color, the more people per square kilometer in that country.

In the 1970s, the United Kingdom, Germany, Italy, Poland and Netherland in Europe, and Japan, Korea, India and Bangladesh in Asia were more densely populated.

By the year 2000, the populations of France, Italy

and other countries in Europe, China, Thailand and Vietnam in Asia, Nigeria in Africa and Cuba and Mexico in South America were also gradually increasing to over 100 people per square kilometer. Specially, population per square kilometer in India reached 322.31.

Finally, Indonesia, the Philippines and Nepal in Asia, Uganda, Ethiopia, and Egypt in Africa, and Austria in Europe also have more than one hundred people per square kilometer in 2020.

But what about the overall population share and growth trends across continents?

Continents

The pie chart shows the population distribution of different continents, select a year in Focused Year selector to display the data of a specific year, click on the sector in the pie chart, and the line graph on the right indicating population growth trend of each continent will show the data of a specific continent.

Because it is the largest, Asia has always accounted for more than half of the world's population, and its

share rose from the 1970s to the beginning of the 20th century, before declining. Europe was the second most populous continent in 1970, but its share continued to decline and was surpassed in 2000 by Africa, which continued to grow in population. The populations of both South and North America are slowly growing, and Oceania's population growth trend is fast, but its population base is so small that it always is the smallest part of the total population.

Next, let's look at the growth rates of the top 8 countries in terms of population.

Growth Rate

The line graph on the right shows the change in growth rate* for the top 10 countries. Please hover your mouse over the chart to see the growth rates of the 8 countries for the current year.

Among the Top 8 countries, both Nigeria and USA maintain population growth rates of around 30% and 10% respectively, and all other countries are gradually or rapidly declining to below 20%.



*Growth Rate = (Past Population - Current Population)/Past Population

First and Second Columns Combined

The line graph on the right shows the change in growth rate ((past population - current population)/past population) for the top 10 countries.
